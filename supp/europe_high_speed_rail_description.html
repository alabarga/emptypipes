The [European high speed rail network map](/2015/05/11/2015-05-12-europe-high-speed-rail/) was made stochastically. Connection information for trains between every city in the [European isochrone maps post](/2015/05/19/2015-05-14-europe-isochrone-map/) and about 50000 other stations in Europe was obtained using the [Swiss public transport API](http://transport.opendata.ch/). This data contained information about train lines as well as the different stops on the route between two cities. Taking the longest route obtained for each train line (i.e. ICE 27, RJ 180, etc...) provided the initial data set. Since this data included many regional, Intercity, and Eurocity trains, the obtained lines were filtered for trains from Spains AVE (Alta Velocidad Española), France's TGV (Train à Grande Vitesse), Germany's ICE (Intercity-Express), Austria's Railjet, Italy's ES (mostly not high speed, but...) and the Eurostar trains.
